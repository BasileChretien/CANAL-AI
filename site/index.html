A Franco-Japanese federated-learning pilot

CANAL-AI

Caen-Nagoya Alliance for AI in Health Data

We are training a shared medical AI across the hospital data warehouses of Caen University Hospital (France) and Nagoya University Hospital (Japan) — predicting cardiovascular adverse events of hormone therapy in prostate cancer, without patient data ever leaving its home country.

Only model parameters cross the border, never patient data.

How it works

A FedBioMed-based federation, realistic by design

  1. 1Each hospital trains a model on its own patient data — locally, inside its own firewall.
  2. 2Only the resulting model parameters are sent to the FedBioMed aggregator in a secure, aggregated form.
  3. 3The aggregator averages parameters across sites and sends the updated model back.
  4. 4Repeat. After many rounds, every hospital holds a model that has learned from all of them — without any patient data ever leaving.

The stack, in plain terms

Primary model
PyTorch multilayer perceptron on OMOP-derived tabular features, trained with FedAvg. FedProx available as a robustness fallback.
Baseline
Federated logistic regression via scikit-learn's FedSGDClassifier.
Privacy layer
Joye-Libert secure aggregation; differential privacy via OPACUS as a sensitivity analysis.
Harmonisation
OMOP-CDM at each site with a custom ETL; publication of this ETL is one of our deliverables.
Explainability
SHAP attributions on each site's held-out cohort, attention-weight inspection, and clinician review.
Endpoint
MACE within 24 months of ADT initiation.
