Franco-Japanese federated learning in health

CANAL-AI

Caen-Nagoya Alliance for AI in Health Data

A federated-learning collaboration predicting cardiovascular adverse events of hormone therapy in prostate cancer, trained across the hospital data warehouses of CHU de Caen (France) and Nagoya University Hospital (Japan).

Only model parameters cross the border — never patient data.

What CANAL-AI does

Androgen deprivation therapy (ADT) is a cornerstone of prostate-cancer management but carries clinically meaningful cardiovascular and metabolic risks. The available agents — GnRH agonists, GnRH antagonists, and androgen-receptor signalling inhibitors — do not share the same cardiovascular risk profile, yet clinicians lack a predictive tool to choose the agent with the best benefit-risk ratio for a given patient.

Single-country models typically fail to generalise across populations with different clinical practices, prescribing patterns, and comorbidity distributions. International data would solve this, but patient-level records cannot cross borders under GDPR (EU) or APPI (Japan). Federated learning is the bridge: model parameters are shared while patient data remain local.

Aims

  1. Primary

    Develop and externally validate a federated, interpretable deep-learning model predicting Major Adverse Cardiovascular Events (MACE) within 24 months of ADT initiation, trained on the union of the CHU de Caen Entrepôt de Données de Santé and the Nagoya University Hospital Data Warehouse.

  2. APPI–GDPR blueprint

    Produce a reusable operational blueprint for Franco-Japanese federated health-data research — beyond CANAL-AI itself.

  3. Benchmark

    Compare privacy-preserving architectures (MLP, TabNet, federated-XGBoost variants) on discrimination, calibration, and cross-site generalisation.

  4. Explainability

    Deliver clinician-facing SHAP / attention-based explanations validated against cardio-oncology expertise.

  5. Extensibility

    Establish a sustainable Caen–Nagoya pipeline, extensible to additional partners (Singapore foreseen for external validation).

Design in brief

Design
Retrospective two-site federated training, prospective external validation.
Cohort
Adult men with confirmed prostate cancer initiating ADT between 2018 and 2025.
Federation
FedBioMed framework, OMOP-CDM for variable harmonisation.
Primary endpoint
MACE within 24 months (composite: myocardial infarction, ischaemic stroke, heart-failure hospitalisation, cardiovascular death).
Secondary endpoints
New-onset diabetes, arterial hypertension, thromboembolic events; drug-specific risk ranking.
Target performance
AUC-ROC ≥ 0.80 on held-out site validation, calibration slope 0.9–1.1.

Data governance

CANAL-AI is designed under the dual constraint of the European GDPR and the Japanese APPI. Patient data never leave the hospital that holds them. Each site trains locally; only updated model parameters are exchanged through a federated aggregator. Ethical review is sought independently at each site (Caen Scientific and Ethics Committee on the French side; Nagoya IRB on the Japanese side).
